Rules of Poker: Texas Hold'em

Objective of the Game

The objective of Texas Hold'em is to win chips by forming the best possible poker hand or by bluffing to make other players fold. The winner is the player with the most chips at the end of the game or the one who wins all the chips during play.

Basic Gameplay

Texas Hold'em uses a standard deck of 52 playing cards. Players are dealt two private cards ("hole cards"), and five community cards are placed face-up on the table. Players use a combination of their hole cards and the community cards to make the best possible five-card poker hand.

Betting Rounds

  • Pre-Flop: Players receive their hole cards and place their initial bets. Betting continues until all players have called or folded.
  • The Flop: Three community cards are dealt face-up. Players bet again, starting with the player to the left of the dealer.
  • The Turn: A fourth community card is dealt face-up. Another round of betting follows.
  • The River: A fifth and final community card is dealt face-up. The final round of betting takes place.

Showdown

If more than one player remains after the final round of betting, a showdown occurs. Players reveal their hole cards, and the best hand wins. The ranking of poker hands from highest to lowest is as follows:

  1. Royal Flush: A, K, Q, J, 10, all of the same suit.
  2. Straight Flush: Five consecutive cards of the same suit.
  3. Four of a Kind: Four cards of the same rank.
  4. Full House: Three of a kind and a pair.
  5. Flush: Five cards of the same suit.
  6. Straight: Five consecutive cards of any suit.
  7. Three of a Kind: Three cards of the same rank.
  8. Two Pair: Two pairs of cards of different ranks.
  9. One Pair: Two cards of the same rank.
  10. High Card: The highest card in a hand without a combination.

Winning and Losing

The winner is the player with the best hand during the showdown or the one who forces all other players to fold by bluffing. The game can end when one player has all the chips or when players decide to end the game.
